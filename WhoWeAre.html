Who We Are

Help Change My City Alliance (HCMC Alliance) is a non-profit organization dedicated to the pursuit of lasting positive change in the lives of multi-barriered (at risk) or homeless youth within Metro Vancouver. The organization’s mission is to empower young people through mentorship, character building, and advanced skill training. HCMC Alliance is committed to equipping youth with employment skills leading to a better and more sustainable future for themselves.

The youth that HCMC Alliance serves need compassion, guidance, and mentorship to help develop them into accomplished and contributing adults. Metro Vancouver has an increasing number of youth who face social barriers that inhibit the opportunities available to them. These youth can easily fall victim to said social barriers which at times may lead to loss of self-value or increased potential for incarceration. The number of youth facing these challenges is increasing and the resources available from the current community organizations are strained.

The programs offered at HCMC Alliance aim to help youth understand their value and develop meaningful relationships with mentors. Using guidance, mentors help them discover life goals and aspirations that will direct their course to fulfill their life purpose. Mentors work alongside youth collaboratively to help develop them wholistically in all the key areas of life. For youth looking for employment we equip them with skills that help them reach their goals of having good work ethic and gainful employment while improving their future, familial ties, and beginning to live out a more positive social impact.

The heart of the organization is to support our youth as friends and family as they begin to stand on their own two feet, overcome life obstacles, and celebrate in their accomplishments. HCMC Alliance welcomes the support of those willing to stand alongside our vision in supporting our youth together.

Designer Bio: Tetyana Golota

Tetyana Golota is an eco-fashion designer and curvy model. She is also an inspirational speaker and the reigning title holder of Mrs. British Columbia. Tetyana was nominated for the Women of Worth and Shakti Award. She is a Hearth & Sole Award Recipient for supporting and inspiring women and has been awarded The Most Inspiring Person Of The Year. Tetyana is also Mrs. Canada Universal and will represent Canada in London this Fall.

Growing up, Tetyana learned how to sew, cross stich, and make patterns from her beloved grandmother. Passionate about eco-couture, her creations were shown at Eco Fashion Week and landed in the pages of Vogue magazine. She is loyal supporter of the Help Change My City Alliance and had her designs shown at the Little Black Dress Gala for the last 3 seasons.

Designer Bio: Joanne Ricci

Giovanna Ricci does not simply create fashion; she creates statement pieces for the strong, confident and self assured woman. Her philosophy is that fashion should be timeless and her designs are relevant both today and tomorrow. Inspiration comes from her Italian culture and the many years she spent working in the fashion industry throughout Italy. Her designs take her on a journey, creating beautiful garments for the evolving woman.

The Rialto Collection to be shown at LBD 5 is named for the famous landmark bridge in Venice. The Rialto collection evokes both the romanticism and the mystery of this majestic city. At night the dark takes one by surprise, losing oneself in the blackened alleyways…only to open up to the grand Rialto Bridge.
